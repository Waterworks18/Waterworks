Hydrological Modelling

We have expertise and experience in rainfall-runoff modelling, hydrological simulation modelling (SHETRAN), FEH statistical modelling, river flow record analysis and low flow analysis. Our hydrological expertise also extends to water balance studies, development of catchment, river and reservoir inflow time series, flow duration and flow frequency analyses, reservoir control rule development and consumptive use estimation. In April 2018 we extensively researched the Morland catchment. This included taking rainfall, potential evaporation, observed flows and elevation data. A simple hydrological bucket model was created from this. The main aim of the project was to assess how landuse change would affect the hydrological system of the catchment. Five different landuse can be altered in the model, including grass, intensive agriculture, forest, bare rock and moorland. By changing the percentage coverage area of these five different landuses, four scenarios were generated for the Morland catchment. Climate change is explored in the rainfall data by studying percentage increases and decreases in the rainfall relative to the observed. The model must be taken with caution as it does not incorporated baseflow, groundwater flow, and changes in potential evaporation. However simple, it displays an understanding of the role of landuse and climate changes on the Morland catchment.
